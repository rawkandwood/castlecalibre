BIO

Hi, my name's Blake.

My background

A Mechanical Engineer by trade, I spent many years designing, operating and maintaining complex engineering systems for the industrial world.

In 2019 my wife and I had our first child. 8 months later, COVID arrived in Australia. Time spent at home suddenly reached unprecedented highs - and so did our energy bills. Why was the house so cold? Could we actually afford to keep it warm?... I became so obsessed that I decided on a career change.

My passion

I consider myself very fortunate to be a homeowner. Transforming our original 1950s weatherboard home into something that I considered appropriate for our young family became more than a necessity... it became my passion.

I retrained in Home Energy Efficiency & Sustainability, became a Certified Passivhaus Consultant and obtained govenrnment-accreditation as a Residential Efficiency Scorecard and Home Energy Rating assessor.

My promise

My focus is to help make your home warmer, drier and use less energy. My service combines not only my technical expertise, but also my practical knowledge and a genuine interest in your wellbeing.

I strive to be at the forefront of industry developments and building science best-practice, so that I can be better informed and offer you the best possible advice for a more liveable home.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a Home Energy Assessment?

A home energy assessment is a comprehensive evaluation of a home’s energy performance. Conducted by an accredited assessor, the process analyses the building fabric or 'thermal envelope' of the home, in addition to the key energy consuming appliances. The habits, behaviours, and energy usage patterns are not under scrutiny. The aim is always to give the occupant the best possible platform, to best support the way they live.

What are the outcomes of a Home Energy Assessment?

Utilising various software and measurement tools, your home is benchmarked according to its overall energy performance. Areas with potential for improvement are identified and paired with tailored, practical recommendations. The result is a clear path towards enhanced comfort, reduced energy bills, and a smaller environmental footprint.

Is retrofitting my home going to cost me a fortune?

To make drastic changes you may need to consider upgrades, but it's not always the big ticket items that make the biggest impact. I take a fabric first approach which strives to minimise energy losses in the first instance - reducing your reliance on those expensive appliances, making them less impactful. Whilst I tailor my advice to suit your situation and match your budget, it's important to remember that every change helps and for each problem there are typically low-cost (or no-cost) solutions that can be implemented.

How long does an assessment take?

The assessment generally takes between 2 and 2.5 hours to complete. This includes plenty of time to hear about your energy-related experiences with the home, to review the assessment once completed, and to discuss your options going forward.

Do you work after hours?

An adult occupant of the home is required to be present during the assessment and as such I make a limited number of time slots available to accommodate after-hours assessments.
